Palak Paneer

Ingredients:

200-250 g paneer (cubed)

250 g fresh spinach (palak)

1 medium onion (finely chopped)

1 medium tomato (chopped)

1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste

1-2 green chilies (optional)

1 teaspoon turmeric powder , 1 teaspoon cumin seeds

1-2 teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to taste)

1 teaspoon garam masala

2 tablespoons cream or 2 tablespoons plain yogurt

1 tablespoon oil or butter

Salt to taste , Water as needed

Instructions:

1.Blanch the spinach: Wash the spinach leaves thoroughly.Boil water in a large pot and add the spinach leaves. Blanch them for 2-3 minutes until wilted.Drain and immediately transfer the spinach to cold water (to retain its green color).Blend the spinach into a smooth paste using a blender.

2.Prepare the masala: Heat oil or butter in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until golden.Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies (if using), and sauté for a minute until fragrant.Stir in the chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, and red chili powder. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil begins to separate from the masala.

3.Add the spinach:Add the spinach puree to the pan and mix well. Add a little water (¼ to ½ cup) to adjust the consistency of the gravy. Let the spinach cook for 4-5 minutes on low heat.

4.Add paneer and finishing touches:Gently stir in the paneer cubes. Add garam masala, salt, and cream or yogurt. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Optionally, add a squeeze of lemon juice for extra freshness.

5.Serve:Garnish with a drizzle of fresh cream if desired. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
